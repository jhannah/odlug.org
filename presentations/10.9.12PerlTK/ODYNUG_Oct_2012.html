What Is Perl/Tk?

Perl/Tk is the combination of the Perl Language, and the Tk widget set originally developed for TCL. There are several alternatives to Tk, but they tend to be buggy, annoying, and difficult. Alternatives include GTK, GTK+, Qt, Tkx (Tk's uses-native-widget cousin),a raw X11 Interface, and WinForms if you are stuck on M$ Windoze.

Why should I use Perl/Tk?

Three Reasons:

  • Simplicity
    • If you can work with OO Perl, you can do just fine with it, it is just OO Perl. You don't have to worry about writing stuff to detect a click, or even a keystroke it a text box, most of it is handled for you, just write a plain-perl callback.
  • Power
    • Because it's simple, it's powerful. You can write something that would take many lines of Perl/GTK code in a few with Tk.
  • Extensibility
    • You can, in general, just inherit a couple of classes, and write a few extra callbacks, and package it up pretty easily, it's a simple process.

Caveat Emptor!

Just a few cautions:

  • Use CPAN to install perl -MCPAN -e "install Tk", do not use the development version.
  • DO NOT use ActiveState Perl, it is a pain to manage, and install anything on, please use Strawberry!
  • Make sure to use Tk rather than Tkx.
    • It uses the "best" looking widget set, which itself can be a bit buggy.
    • Most people on PerlMonks know Tk better, very few know Tkx.
